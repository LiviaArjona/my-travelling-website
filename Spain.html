Málaga

Malaga is a city located in the southern region of Spain, on the Costa del Sol. The area has been inhabited since prehistoric times, with evidence of settlements dating back to the Bronze Age. Over the centuries, the region has been ruled by various civilizations, each of which has left its mark on the city.

  • Main tourist areas that I liked the most:
    • 1. Alcazaba of Málaga: The Alcazaba is a palatial fortification built in the early 11th century by the Hammudid dynasty. It offers stunning views of the city and the sea, along with beautiful gardens and impressive Moorish architecture.
    • 2. Castillo de Gibralfaro Located adjacent to the Alcazaba, this castle dates back to the 14th century. It is perched on a hill and offers panoramic views of Málaga and its port. The walk up to the castle is steep but rewarding.
    • 3. Málaga Cathedral (La Manquita) The Renaissance-style cathedral, known as La Manquita (the One-Armed Lady) due to its unfinished second tower, is a significant landmark. Its interior is just as impressive as its exterior, with beautiful chapels and a stunning choir.
  • The average daily spend for a tourist in Malaga
    • Accommodation: €20 - €40 (Hostels or budget hotels)
    • Food: €15 - €25 (Eating at budget-friendly restaurants, tapas bars, or self-catering)
    • Transportation: €5 - €10 (Public transport or walking)
    • Activities: €5 - €15 (Visiting free or low-cost attractions, parks, and beaches)
  • Total: €45 - €90 per day
