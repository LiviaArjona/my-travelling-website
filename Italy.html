ITALY

Venice

Venice, Italy's enchanting city of canals, boasts a rich tapestry of history and culture. Founded in the 5th century AD, it rose to prominence as a maritime powerhouse, dominating trade routes and amassing great wealth. The Venetian Republic, with its unique political system, thrived for over a thousand years, leaving behind a legacy of architectural marvels and artistic masterpieces from the Renaissance era. Today, Venice faces modern challenges such as rising sea levels and overtourism, yet its timeless allure continues to beckon visitors from across the globe, drawn to its romantic waterways, historic landmarks, and enduring charm.

  • Main tourist areas that I liked the most:
    • 1. St. Mark's Square (Piazza San Marco): The principal square of Venice, surrounded by iconic landmarks such as St. Mark's Basilica, the Doge's Palace, and the Campanile (bell tower). Known for its elegant architecture, outdoor cafes, and lively atmosphere.
    • 2. Grand Canal (Canal Grande): Venice's main waterway, lined with stunning palaces, churches, and historic buildings. A vaporetto (water bus) ride along the Grand Canal offers panoramic views of the city's landmarks.
    • 3. Rialto Bridge (Ponte di Rialto): One of the most famous bridges in Venice, spanning the Grand Canal. Known for its elegant design and bustling market area, offering a variety of shops and stalls.
  • The average daily spend for a tourist in Venice
    • Accommodation: €50 - €100 (Hostels or budget hotels)
    • Food: €20 - €40 (Eating at budget-friendly restaurants, street food, or self-catering)
    • Transportation: €10 - €20 (Vaporetto passes for water buses or walking)
    • Activities: €10 - €20 (Visiting free attractions, walking tours, or museum entrance fees)
  • Total: €90 - €180 per day
