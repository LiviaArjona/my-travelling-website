POLAND

Kraków

Kraków, Poland's historic gem, brims with medieval charm and cultural riches. Its UNESCO-listed Old Town boasts architectural marvels like Wawel Castle and St. Mary's Basilica. Home to Poland's oldest university, Kraków pulses with intellectual vitality. Culinary delights abound, from hearty pierogi to sweet obwarzanek krakowski. The city's Jewish quarter, Kazimierz, is a testament to its diverse heritage. Surrounded by scenic beauty, Kraków invites exploration of both its storied past and vibrant present.

  • Main tourist areas that I liked the most:
    • 1. Old Town (Stare Miasto): The heart of Kraków, featuring medieval architecture, cobblestone streets, and charming squares. Highlights include the Main Market Square (Rynek Główny), St. Mary's Basilica, and the Cloth Hall (Sukiennice).
    • 2. Wawel Hill: Home to the historic Wawel Castle and Wawel Cathedral, where Polish kings were crowned and buried. Visitors can explore the royal chambers, treasury, and dragon's den, as well as enjoy panoramic views of the city from the hilltop.
    • 3. Planty Park: A green oasis encircling the Old Town, created on the site of former city walls. Perfect for leisurely walks, picnics, and enjoying views of historic landmarks.
  • The average daily spend for a tourist in Kraków
    • Accommodation: €20 - €50 (Hostels or budget guesthouses)
    • Food: €10 - €20 (Eating at inexpensive restaurants, street food, or self-catering)
    • Transportation: €2 - €5 (Using public transportation, walking, or biking)
    • Activities: €5 - €15 (Visiting free attractions, self-guided walking tours, or enjoying parks)
  • Total: €37 - €90 per day
