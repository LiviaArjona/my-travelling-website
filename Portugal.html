PORTUGAL

Lisboa

Lisbon, Portugal's radiant capital, captivates with its colorful streets, rich history, and vibrant culture. Perched on the edge of the Atlantic Ocean, Lisbon boasts a picturesque setting of hills, offering panoramic views of the Tagus River. Its historic neighborhoods, such as Alfama and Bairro Alto, reveal layers of Moorish and medieval influences, while elegant plazas and grand monuments narrate tales of Portugal's illustrious past. From savoring pastéis de nata in quaint cafes to exploring ancient castles and contemporary art galleries, Lisbon invites visitors to immerse themselves in its captivating blend of tradition and modernity, making it a truly unforgettable destination.

  • Main tourist areas that I liked the most:
    • 1. Parque Eduardo VII: A sprawling park offering panoramic views of the city and the Tagus River. Visitors can relax amidst lush greenery, visit the greenhouse, and admire the Monument to the Carnation Revolution.
    • 2. Baixa District: The heart of Lisbon's historic center, featuring elegant squares, grand avenues, and iconic landmarks. Highlights include Praça do Comércio (Commerce Square), Rossio Square, and Rua Augusta Arch.
    • 3. Belém: A riverside district known for its rich maritime heritage and iconic monuments. Must-see attractions include Belém Tower, Jerónimos Monastery, and the Monument to the Discoveries.
  • The average daily spend for a tourist in Lisboa
    • Accommodation: €20 - €50 (Hostels or budget guesthouses)
    • Food: €10 - €20 (Eating at inexpensive restaurants, street food, or self-catering)
    • Transportation: €2 - €7 (Using public transportation, such as buses, trams, and metro)
    • Activities: €5 - €15 (Visiting free attractions, self-guided walking tours, or enjoying parks)
  • Total: €37 - €92 per day
